## Background & Business Purpose

Vertex Metals Ltd is being established as a commodity and metals sourcing intermediary, incorporated on the Isle of Man. The Company acts as principal in trades — sourcing materials from qualified international suppliers and supplying UK industrial buyers under documented contracts — rather than holding physical stock on its own account.

### Business model summary

The Company is a sourcing desk, not a single-product importer. UK industrial buyers approach Vertex Metals for their metals and mineral requirements; Vertex Metals draws on a global supplier network, qualifies suppliers against its AML/KYC and quality standards, negotiates competitive FOB pricing, and delivers CIF/DDP to the UK buyer under a single, compliant counterparty relationship.

Vertex Metals takes title to goods during transit, manages all trade documentation, and earns a margin on the spread between FOB purchase and UK delivered sale prices. Settlement is exclusively by bank transfer or Irrevocable Documentary Letter of Credit — the Company operates a strict no-cash policy.

### Key founding assets

**Jackson Paul** — brings extensive supplier relationships across multiple metals and mineral categories, developed over years of sole-proprietor metals trading sourcing for UK and European buyers from international mills and producers. His network spans manufacturing clusters in India, Europe, and East Asia, and includes smelter, mill, and mine-side contacts across aluminium, copper, stainless steel, and broader industrial metals. Jackson has prior engagement with the UK Chamber of Commerce on lithium import supply chain discussions. He leads supplier relationships, UK buyer development, and commercial deal execution.

**Martyn Bourner** — brings a background as a qualified airline pilot, a discipline that translates directly into the structured decision-making, procedural rigour, and regulatory awareness required of a compliant trading business. Alongside aviation, Martyn has built bespoke management systems across multiple sectors and has direct expertise in the air transport of dangerous goods — a capability directly applicable as the Company expands into lithium battery precursors and other hazardous mineral shipments. Martyn leads operations, technology and systems, compliance (including MLRO responsibilities), financial reporting, and logistics.

## Sourcing Capability

Vertex Metals positions as a global sourcing desk for UK industrial buyers. The Company's capability is organised into three tiers: an active launch product driving initial revenue, a core sourcing capability across established metals families, and a forward-capability in critical minerals and mineral concentrates supplied on request.

### Launch product (active from trading commencement — first 6 months focus)

**Aluminium Alloy Core Wire — EC Grade and 6XXX series**, sourced from manufacturing clusters in Gujarat (Ahmedabad) and Odisha, India.

Rationale for launch selection:
- India's market share in UK aluminium wire grew 2,615% year-on-year
- Aluminium designated a UK Critical Mineral (March 2025)
- Strong demand from West Midlands automotive (EV cabling), aerospace wiring, and energy infrastructure sectors
- India–UK CETA (May 2025) provides near tariff-free access
- Moderate technical barrier justifies an intermediary presence
- Jackson's existing Indian manufacturing network is directly applicable

This is the focus of initial marketing, lead development, and first trades. The broader sourcing capability below is available from day one but is not the focus of initial commercial activity.

### Core sourcing capability (live from day one; supplied on request against buyer enquiry)

Vertex Metals sources across four principal metal families, drawing on established mill and manufacturer relationships across India, Europe, East Asia, and selectively elsewhere. Where a specific corridor offers structural commercial advantage — notably the India–UK CETA for India-origin goods, and the UK's CPTPP and UK–Chile Association Agreement for South American sourcing — Vertex Metals structures trades to capitalise on that advantage. Where a different origin better serves the buyer's specification, price, or lead-time needs, Vertex Metals sources accordingly.

**Aluminium Alloys**
Forms: bars, plates, coils, rods, pipes, sheets, tube, channel, I-beam, T-profile, wire.
Primary origin: India (Gujarat, Odisha). Secondary origins: Europe, China.

**Copper and Copper Products**
Forms: plates, rods, wire, tube, sheets, pipes.
Primary origin: India. Secondary origins: under development (Chilean copper concentrate evaluated under CPTPP framework as part of Phase 2).

**Stainless Steel**
Forms: bars, sheets, plates, coils, rods, pipes, wire, tube, channel, I-beam, T-profile.
Origins: leading mills across India, Europe, and East Asia.

**Other Metals**
Including: brass, bronze, tool steel, bright steel, titanium (non-India origin, subject to sanctions and origin verification), nickel superalloys (including Inconel grades), and other specialist metals on request.
Origins: global, sourced against specific buyer specifications.

### Forward capability — Critical Minerals and Mineral Concentrates (on enquiry)

Vertex Metals markets a forward sourcing capability across critical minerals and mineral concentrates, recognising the UK's Vision 2035 Critical Minerals Strategy and the substantial projected demand growth across this category. These materials are not part of day-one active inventory or committed supply but are available to source on enquiry from the Company's qualified supplier network:

- **Copper concentrate** — South American and Indian origin under development
- **Lithium-ion battery precursor materials** — including lithium carbonate; sourced via established producers in South America and, as Indian refining capacity matures, India
- **Mica** — India is the global leader; Vertex Metals sources only from suppliers with certified responsible-sourcing credentials (Responsible Mica Initiative or equivalent)
- **Manganese ore** — Indian production clusters in Odisha and Maharashtra
- **Chromite / chromium** — India is the world's second-largest producer
- **Bauxite** — Indian and other established origins

These forward capabilities are publicly advertised to ensure UK buyers with medium-term critical-minerals requirements engage Vertex Metals at enquiry stage rather than qualifying the Company out due to a narrow visible offering. Each enquiry is assessed against the Company's AML/KYC framework, ESG standards, and supplier-qualification process before any commitment is made.

## Commercial Positioning

Vertex Metals competes on four core differentiators, not on price alone:

1. **Compliance as a standard, not an afterthought.** AML/KYC, sanctions screening, ESG documentation, and CBAM readiness are built into every trade from onboarding onwards. UK procurement, legal, and ESG teams can trade with confidence.

2. **Trade-agreement expertise.** The Company actively structures trades to leverage the UK's network of trade agreements — most notably the India–UK CETA, but also the UK's CPTPP membership and bilateral agreements with Chile and other partners — to deliver structural tariff and landed-cost advantages to UK buyers.

3. **Global sourcing with local accountability.** Buyers deal with a single Isle of Man counterparty, not a chain of foreign intermediaries. All payment, documentation, and contractual recourse sits with Vertex Metals Ltd.

4. **Sourcing breadth with technical credibility.** The Company can source across metal families and product forms, backed by founder expertise in both commercial metals trading (Jackson) and operational/regulatory disciplines including dangerous goods transport (Martyn).

## Compliance Framework (Summary)

Vertex Metals operates under an Anti-Money Laundering, Counter-Terrorist Financing and KYC policy that is currently being rewritten to reflect the multi-geography, multi-product sourcing model. The revised policy will address:

- **Tiered onboarding**: standard, enhanced, and simplified CDD based on counterparty, geography, product, and transaction value
- **Geography-based risk weighting** aligned with FATF guidance and the UK National Risk Assessment
- **Product-category risk tiers** acknowledging that aluminium commercial forms, mineral concentrates, precious metals, and critical minerals each carry distinct risk profiles
- **Sanctions screening** at onboarding, every six months thereafter, and immediately on any relevant new designation
- **ESG and responsible-sourcing requirements**, including mandatory certified sourcing for mica and documented due diligence for copper, lithium, and any mineral with heightened reputational risk
- **No-cash policy**, with all settlements by bank transfer or Irrevocable Documentary Letter of Credit
- **CBAM readiness** for aluminium (and, when in scope, other materials) ahead of the 1 January 2027 UK commencement date

MLRO responsibility sits with Martyn Bourner; Deputy MLRO responsibility with Jackson Paul. All SARs are routed via the IOM Financial Intelligence Unit (THEMIS) and/or UK National Crime Agency as applicable.

The updated policy will be finalised and adopted at incorporation.

## Key Risks & Mitigations (Summary)

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Director benefits declaration trigger at incorporation | Timeline deliberately planned around this; pre-incorporation work handled by Martyn personally as planning activity |
| Bank reluctance for metals-trading businesses with pre-revenue model | Front-load AML policy, business plan, supplier framework, and governance documentation before first approach; target banks experienced with IOM-incorporated trading companies |
| Supplier/buyer disintermediation ("cut-out" risk) | Positioning as compliance-and-documentation partner rather than pure price arbitrage; long-term buyer relationships built around service quality; appropriate contractual protections |
| Sanctions exposure on multi-geography sourcing (particularly copper, titanium, certain nickel grades) | Strict sanctions screening at onboarding and pre-trade; origin verification required; MLRO approval for any counterparty in or connected to higher-risk jurisdictions |
| ESG reputational risk, particularly mica and cobalt-adjacent materials | Certified responsible-sourcing only (e.g., Responsible Mica Initiative); documented supplier ESG due diligence; public responsible-sourcing policy |
| Indian export controls on specific minerals (titanium/atomic minerals, variable duty schedules) | DGFT schedule monitored; titanium sourced non-India; flexibility written into supplier contracts |
| CBAM exposure on aluminium from 1 January 2027 | Supplier carbon-intensity data collection in progress; CBAM authorised-declarant registration planned; preference for verified lower-intensity suppliers |
| Currency volatility (GBP/INR, GBP/USD, GBP/EUR) | Short-term contracts initially; forward contracts or LC-fixed pricing for larger trades as volume develops |
